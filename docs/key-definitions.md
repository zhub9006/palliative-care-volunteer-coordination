# Key Definitions

## Core Terms

- **Hospice and Palliative Care (HPC)** — An approach that improves the quality of life of patients and their families facing life-threatening illness, through the prevention and relief of suffering.

- **Volunteer** — An individual who freely chooses to contribute time, energy, and skills to hospice and palliative care without financial compensation, operating under the guidance of the care organization.

- **Volunteer Coordination** — The systematic management of volunteer recruitment, training, deployment, supervision, and recognition within a palliative care setting.

- **Volunteer Services Manager (VSM)** — A designated staff member responsible for coordinating all aspects of the volunteer program.

- **Community-Based Palliative Care (CBPC)** — Palliative care delivered in community settings (homes, community centers) rather than hospitals, often relying heavily on volunteers.

- **Scope of Practice** — The defined boundaries of what a volunteer may and may not do within a palliative care setting.

- **Formal Volunteering** — Structured volunteer activity within an organization, with defined roles, training, and supervision.

- **Informal Volunteering** — Spontaneous, community-driven support that is not organized through a formal institution.

- **Accreditation** — A formal process by which an authorized body evaluates and recognizes a palliative care program as meeting established standards.

- **Norms of Practice** — Established principles and standards that guide the delivery of hospice palliative care, including volunteer involvement.

- **Accompagnement (French)** — The French concept of "accompanying" a person at end of life, emphasizing presence and companionship over intervention.

- **Acompañamiento (Spanish)** — The Spanish equivalent of accompaniment, central to volunteer identity in palliative care.

- **Begleitung (German)** — The German term for accompanying, a core concept in hospice volunteering emphasizing being present with the dying person.

- **Compassionate Community** — A public health approach where communities take collective responsibility for end-of-life care, exemplified by the Kerala model in India.

- **Integrated Community Palliative Care (ICPC)** — A family-centered approach linking formal health systems with community-level services (used in South Africa and other African contexts).
