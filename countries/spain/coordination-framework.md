# Spain: Palliative Care Volunteer Coordination

## National Framework

Spain's volunteer coordination in palliative care operates under:

- **Estrategia Nacional de Cuidados Paliativos** (National Palliative Care Strategy, MSSSI)
- **SECPAL** (Sociedad Española de Cuidados Paliativos): National professional society
- **Regional health laws**: Spain's decentralized health system means coordination varies significantly by autonomous community
- **Ley de Voluntariado** (Volunteer Law, 2015): National framework for volunteering

### Governance Structure
- **Ministry of Health (MSSSI)**: Sets national strategy
- **Autonomous communities**: 17 regions with their own health systems — primary implementation
- **SECPAL**: Professional guidelines and standards
- **Local associations and NGOs**: Many hospice and palliative care associations operate at regional/local level

### Legal Context
- **Ley 45/2015 de Voluntariado**: National volunteer law defining rights, duties, and protections
- **Regional health laws**: Each autonomous community has its own palliative care provisions
- **Ley 41/2002 (Autonomía del Paciente)**: Patient autonomy law including end-of-life provisions
- **Volunteer protection**: Law 45/2015 provides liability protection for volunteers

## Volunteer Operational Model

### The Spanish Acompañamiento Model

Spain shares the **Acompañamiento** philosophy with France, but with a more decentralized implementation:

1. **Recruitment**: Through local palliative care associations, hospital volunteer programs, church groups
2. **Training**: 20–30 hours, covering:
   - Acompañamiento philosophy
   - Communication and listening skills
   - Duelo (grief) and bereavement
   - Teamwork with healthcare professionals
   - Cultural and spiritual sensitivity
   - Self-care
3. **Deployment**: Volunteers in hospitals, palliative care units, and home care programs
4. **Supervision**: Varies by region — some have formal supervision, others more informal
5. **Recognition**: Regional volunteer recognition events; ongoing training opportunities

### Role Categories

| Role | Activities |
|------|------------|
| Acompañante (accompaniment volunteer) | Being present, listening, emotional support |
| Home care volunteer | Home visits, practical support, family respite |
| Bereavement support | Duelo groups, individual follow-up |
| Administrative | Association management, fundraising, awareness campaigns |

### Distinctive Features
- **Decentralized implementation**: Significant variation across 17 autonomous communities
- **Catholic influence**: Many volunteer programs rooted in Catholic organizations (Cáritas, Confer)
- **Growing palliative care movement**: SECPAL and regional societies expanding rapidly
- **Home care emphasis**: Strong tradition of home-based accompaniment
- **EU-funded projects**: Spain participated in Europall project comparing 7 EU countries

## Quality Standards

- SECPAL guidelines for palliative care practice
- Estrategia Nacional quality indicators
- Regional health authority standards
- Ley de Voluntariado compliance requirements

## Key Challenges

1. **Decentralization**: Inconsistent standards and coordination across regions
2. **Resource limitations**: Many regions lack formal palliative care programs
3. **Recognition gap**: Volunteer accompaniment not always recognized by healthcare system
4. **Training inconsistency**: No national standardized volunteer training curriculum
5. **Association sustainability**: Small local associations struggle with funding
