# India: Palliative Care Volunteer Coordination

## National Framework

India's volunteer coordination in palliative care is uniquely **community-driven**, with the **Kerala model** recognized globally as a best practice:

- **National Programme for Palliative Care (NPPC)**, 2012 — Government of India policy framework
- **Indian Association of Palliative Care (IAPC)** — National professional body
- **Pallium India** — Major NGO and training institution based in Kerala
- **State-level policies**: Vary dramatically — Kerala is a pioneer; many states have minimal infrastructure

### Governance Structure
- **Ministry of Health and Family Welfare**: National policy
- **State health departments**: Implementation (highly variable)
- **IAPC**: Standards, training, accreditation
- **Pallium India**: Community engagement, training, advocacy
- **Neighbourhood Network in Palliative Care (NNPC)**: Kerala's community-based model

### Legal Context
- **No specific national palliative care legislation**
- **Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act (1985)**: Historically restricted morphine access; amended in 2014
- **State-level palliative care policies**: Kerala was first to adopt (2008)
- **National Programme for Palliative Care (2012)**: Policy framework but not legally binding
- **Volunteer protection**: Limited formal legal protection

## Volunteer Operational Model

### The Kerala Community-Based Model (NNPC)

Kerala's **Neighbourhood Network in Palliative Care (NNPC)** is the world's largest community-based palliative care network:

1. **Recruitment**: Community mobilization — student groups, women's collectives, local leaders
2. **Training**: 10–30 hours (variable), covering:
   - Basic palliative care concepts
   - Home-based care skills
   - Community mobilization
   - Communication and listening
   - Basic nursing assistance
   - Spiritual and cultural sensitivity
3. **Deployment**: Volunteers provide care in homes, community centers, and outpatient clinics
4. **Supervision**: Local volunteer coordinators; monthly review meetings
5. **Recognition**: Community celebrations; local media; annual conferences

### The Pallium India Model

Pallium India's approach to volunteer engagement:
- Connects with student-activist groups (e.g., Coastal Students' Cultural Forum)
- Trains volunteers in basic palliative care awareness
- Creates local volunteer networks linked to palliative care clinics
- Emphasizes community ownership and sustainability

### Role Categories

| Role | Activities |
|------|------------|
| Community volunteer | Home visits, emotional support, practical assistance |
| Health worker volunteer | Basic care tasks, medication reminders, wound care assistance |
| Community mobilizer | Awareness campaigns, recruitment, fundraising |
| Administrative | Record keeping, coordination, communication |
| Student volunteer | Awareness, peer education, community engagement |

### Distinctive Features
- **Volunteers as primary care providers**: In many areas, volunteers are the only source of palliative care
- **Compassionate Community approach**: Entire neighborhoods take responsibility for end-of-life care
- **Student engagement**: Universities and colleges as volunteer recruitment channels
- **Low-cost, high-impact**: Model designed for resource-limited settings
- **Cultural adaptation**: Deeply rooted in local community structures and cultural practices

## Quality Standards

- IAPC standards and guidelines
- NNPC quality framework (Kerala)
- Pallium India training curriculum
- State-level palliative care policy indicators (where they exist)

## Key Challenges

1. **Access**: Palliative care reaches only ~1% of the Indian population
2. **Urban-rural divide**: Services concentrated in urban tertiary hospitals
3. **No national legislation**: Voluntary policy framework with limited enforcement
4. **Funding**: Minimal government funding; reliance on donations and international grants
5. **Opioid access**: Despite 2014 amendments, morphine availability remains problematic
6. **Scale**: Replicating the Kerala model across India's diverse states is extremely challenging
