# Ireland: Palliative Care Volunteer Coordination

## National Framework

Ireland has a structured national approach to palliative care with clear clinical governance:

- **HSE National Clinical Programme for Palliative Care: Model of Care** — Comprehensive national model
- **Palliative Care for All** — National advisory committee guidance
- **Irish Hospice Foundation (IHF)** — Major national charity providing leadership
- **HIQA (Health Information and Quality Authority)** — National quality standards

### Governance Structure
- **HSE (Health Service Executive)**: National health service — implements palliative care policy
- **National Clinical Programme for Palliative Care**: Clinical leadership and model of care
- **IHF**: Advocacy, education, and program development
- **Local hospice organizations**: Manage volunteer programs (Our Lady's Hospice, Milford Care Centre, etc.)

### Legal Context
- **Health Act 2004**: Establishes HSE
- **National Standards for Safer Better Healthcare** (HIQA): Include palliative care components
- **Assisted Decision-Making (Capacity) Act 2015**: Affects end-of-life decision making
- **Volunteer protection**: Limited specific legislation

## Volunteer Operational Model

### The Irish Hospice Model

Ireland's model is **formal and integrated**, with strong clinical governance:

1. **Recruitment**: Through local hospices, IHF, community outreach
2. **Training**: 20–30 hours, covering:
   - Palliative care philosophy
   - Communication and listening skills
   - Bereavement and grief
   - Boundaries and self-care
   - Cultural and spiritual sensitivity
   - HSE governance requirements
3. **Deployment**: Volunteers in hospices, hospitals, and community settings
4. **Supervision**: Dedicated volunteer coordinators; regular supervision sessions
5. **Recognition**: National Volunteering Week; hospice-specific events; ongoing education

### Role Categories

| Role | Activities |
|------|------------|
| Hospice volunteer | Companionship, emotional support, practical assistance |
| Home care volunteer | Home visits, family support, respite |
| Bereavement support | Grief support, telephone follow-up, groups |
| Day care volunteer | Activity facilitation, social engagement |
| Administrative | Fundraising, hospice shops, events |

### Distinctive Features
- **Strong clinical governance**: HSE Model of Care provides clear framework
- **IHF leadership**: Irish Hospice Foundation plays major coordinating role
- **Hospice shop tradition**: Similar to UK — major fundraising channel
- **Compact size**: Easier national coordination
- **Community palliative care**: Growing emphasis on home-based care

## Quality Standards

- HIQA National Standards
- HSE Model of Care quality indicators
- IHF quality benchmarks
- Volunteering Ireland standards

## Key Challenges

1. **Rural access**: Challenges in remote rural areas, especially in the west
2. **Funding**: Reliance on charitable fundraising alongside HSE funding
3. **Workforce**: Competition for volunteers with other healthcare charities
4. **Integration**: Ensuring volunteer coordination aligns with HSE clinical governance
