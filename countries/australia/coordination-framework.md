# Australia: Palliative Care Volunteer Coordination

## National Framework

Australia's volunteer coordination in palliative care is guided by:

- **National Palliative Care Strategy** (2018)
- **National Palliative Care Standards** (5th edition, Palliative Care Australia)
- **Australian Commission on Safety and Quality in Health Care (ACSQHC)** standards
- **State/territory-specific programs** (e.g., NSW Palliative Care Volunteer Services Support Program)

### Governance Structure
- **Federal**: Department of Health and Aged Care — national strategy and funding
- **State/Territory**: Health departments implement and fund state-specific programs
- **Palliative Care Australia (PCA)**: National peak body
- **ACSQHC**: Sets national safety and quality standards

### Legal Context
- No single federal palliative care act
- State/territory legislation governs health services
- Volunteer protection varies by jurisdiction
- Aged Care Quality Standards apply to volunteers in aged care settings

## Volunteer Operational Model

### The Australian Model

Australia's model is **formal and service-integrated**, similar to Canada:

1. **Recruitment**: Community advertising, online platforms, partnerships with community organizations
2. **Training**: 20–30 hours typically, covering:
   - Palliative care philosophy
   - Communication and listening skills
   - Grief, loss, and bereavement
   - Self-care and resilience
   - Cultural awareness (including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives)
   - Infection control and safety
3. **Deployment**: Coordinated by state-level programs (e.g., Palliative Care NSW)
4. **Supervision**: Regular support sessions; debriefing opportunities
5. **Recognition**: National Volunteer Week; ongoing education; peer networks

### NSW Volunteer Services Support Program

A notable state-level initiative:
- Supports palliative care volunteer services across NSW
- Provides education, training, and networking for volunteers
- Promotes best practice in volunteer management
- Partners with Palliative Care NSW and local health districts

### Distinctive Features
- **Aboriginal Health Workers**: Integration of Indigenous health perspectives
- **Rural and remote focus**: Specialized approaches for remote communities
- **Aged care integration**: Volunteers increasingly active in residential aged care

## Quality Standards

- Palliative Care Australia National Standards
- ACSQHC National Safety and Quality Health Service Standards
- Volunteering Australia's National Standards for Volunteer Involvement

## Key Challenges

1. Geographic challenges: Vast distances, remote community access
2. Cultural competency: Engaging Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities
3. Workforce shortages: Particularly in rural and remote areas
4. Funding sustainability: Reliance on short-term grants
